EASY LEVEL – 5 Questions

Q1. Name the scientist who first experimentally demonstrated the existence of electromagnetic waves in 1885.

Q2. Define displacement current and write its mathematical expression in terms of the rate of change of electric flux.

Q3. What is the fundamental source of electromagnetic waves according to Maxwell’s theory?

Q4. State the approximate range of wavelengths for the electromagnetic spectrum, from gamma rays to long radio waves, as described in the text.

Q5. Write the generalized form of Ampere’s circuital law, also known as the Ampere-Maxwell law.

MEDIUM LEVEL – 5 Questions

Q1. Explain why stationary charges and charges in uniform motion cannot be sources of electromagnetic waves.

Q2. Describe the "logical inconsistency" Maxwell found in Ampere’s circuital law when applying it to a charging capacitor.

Q3. "Time-dependent electric and magnetic fields give rise to each other." Justify this statement with reference to Faraday’s law and the Ampere-Maxwell law.

Q4. A parallel plate capacitor is being charged. Compare the conduction current and the displacement current (a) outside the capacitor plates and (b) inside the region between the capacitor plates.

Q5. Why is the experimental demonstration of light as an electromagnetic wave not possible using current generated by modern electronic circuits? Mention the typical frequencies involved.

HARD LEVEL – 5 Questions

Q1. For a parallel plate capacitor with area $A$ and charge $Q$, show that the displacement current $i_d$ is exactly equal to the conduction current $i = \frac{dQ}{dt}$ flowing in the connecting wires.

Q2. State Maxwell’s four equations in vacuum in integral form. Explain the physical significance of the term $\mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt}$ in the fourth equation.

Q3. An electromagnetic wave is produced by a charge oscillating with a specific frequency. If the wavelength of the produced wave is $10^{-12}$ m, identify the type of wave and calculate its frequency. (Take $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s).

Q4. Using a circular loop of radius $r$ centered symmetrically around a current-carrying wire, show how the magnetic field $B$ is calculated at a point $P$ outside a capacitor, and explain why the result remains consistent regardless of the shape of the surface bounded by the loop.

Q5. Discuss the symmetry between electricity and magnetism introduced by Maxwell's displacement current. In what way are the laws of electricity and magnetism still not "perfectly symmetrical"?
